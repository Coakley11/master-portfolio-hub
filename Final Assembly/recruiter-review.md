# Recruiter Review — Daniel Cohen Portfolio

**Perspective:** Five target hiring personas reviewing the assembled portfolio (structure, copy, evidence — screenshots pending).

**Positioning anchor:** Mathematics & Statistics Professional · Data Analyst · Quantitative Educator · AI-Assisted Application Developer.

---

## 1. AI Evaluator

### Strengths
- Applied Mathematical Intelligence lab includes explicit **AI training / overfitting** demo — directly relevant to model evaluation literacy.
- SQL & Excel **AI Evaluator workbook** listed with dedicated showcase path.
- Case-study structure (Problem → Data → Analysis → Modeling → Insights → Results) mirrors evaluation reporting discipline.
- Seven-app suite shows breadth of AI-assisted product development without overclaiming production ML ops.

### Weaknesses
- No published write-up of a formal **evaluation rubric**, benchmark suite, or human-in-the-loop scoring pipeline.
- Music and NBA apps emphasize UX over model cards / failure-mode documentation.

### Missing evidence
- Side-by-side model comparison table (accuracy, latency, cost, bias notes).
- Red-team or adversarial test examples.
- Annotation guidelines or inter-rater reliability sample.

### Recommended resume emphasis
Lead with **AI Evaluator Analytics workbook**, **Train an AI** overfitting module, and **structured case studies**. Add one bullet on designing repeatable evaluation workflows (metrics + validation split + interpretability).

## 2. Data Analyst

### Strengths
- **Strongest persona fit.** Investment and Baseball apps demonstrate SQL-adjacent analytics, significance testing, dashboards, and KPI storytelling.
- Skills dashboard (Python, SQL, Pandas, Statistics, Excel) aligns with typical DA job posts.
- Four flagship case studies with business-value paragraphs and recruiter takeaways.
- Live Streamlit demos reduce friction for hiring managers.

### Weaknesses
- Screenshots not yet visible — first impression currently shows placeholders.
- Limited explicit mention of **dbt / warehouse / scheduled ETL** (understandable for portfolio scope).

### Missing evidence
- One end-to-end **SQL query sample** or data dictionary excerpt on the site.
- Stakeholder-facing slide or email summary mock (beyond Streamlit).

### Recommended resume emphasis
Quantify outcomes where possible (e.g., “built portfolio risk dashboard with Sharpe, Monte Carlo, efficient frontier”). Pair **Investment** + **Baseball comparison testing** + **SQL workbooks** as a DA trifecta.

## 3. Business Intelligence Analyst

### Strengths
- Executive-summary language in project copy (`executiveSummary`, `businessValue`, `recruiterTakeaway`).
- Dashboard-first apps (Investment overview, NBA home command center, SQL Excel dashboards).
- Featured Analytics section maps methods to business decisions (optimization, scenario simulation, visualization).

### Weaknesses
- No dedicated **Power BI / Tableau** artifact (Streamlit-heavy narrative).
- Command Center and Future Lens are lower readiness — may dilute focus if featured equally with Tier 1.

### Missing evidence
- Sample **executive one-pager** PDF or static report export.
- KPI definition table (metric name, formula, grain, refresh cadence).

### Recommended resume emphasis
Frame Streamlit builds as **self-service BI layers** with demo modes for executive walkthroughs. Highlight Investment health scoring and NBA series probability as decision-support KPIs.

## 4. Research Analyst

### Strengths
- Hypothesis testing, Welch tests, p-values (Baseball).
- Monte Carlo, efficient frontier, macro scenario framing (Investment).
- SIR disease model and betting EV (Applied Math) show theoretical grounding.
- Case-study methodology blocks support research-style communication.

### Weaknesses
- Citations / data provenance not surfaced on the marketing site (may live in app READMEs).
- Academic-style reproducibility (notebooks, DOIs) not highlighted.

### Missing evidence
- Short **methods appendix** per flagship project (sample size, assumptions, limitations).
- Link to a static PDF or Jupyter export for one analysis.

### Recommended resume emphasis
Lead with **statistical testing**, **simulation design**, and **model assumptions**. Use Applied Math disease spread + Baseball significance + Investment Monte Carlo as a research-methods narrative.

## 5. Junior Data Scientist

### Strengths
- ML projections with feature importance (Baseball).
- Portfolio optimization and correlation modeling (Investment).
- Multiple deployed interactive models (not just Kaggle notebooks).
- Experimentation pillar and AI training visualization.

### Weaknesses
- No classic **scikit-learn / XGBoost** project card with cross-validated metrics on the homepage.
- Deep learning references are light; music AI coaching is product-oriented.

### Missing evidence
- Confusion matrix, ROC, or MAE/RMSE table for one ML model.
- Train/validation/test split methodology called out on-site.

### Recommended resume emphasis
Baseball **ML Predictions** + Investment **optimizer / Monte Carlo** + Applied Math **overfitting demo**. Add one bullet on feature engineering and model validation discipline.
